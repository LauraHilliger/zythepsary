Webmaking / web literacy is a set of skills that prepare and empower people to use the web for their own & others' benefit.


Our initial definition of web literacy includes skills that span:


Exploring the web (browser basics, searching, etc.)

Authoring content (basic HTML, linking, remixing, etc.)

Connecting with people online (sharing, collaborative making, etc.)

Building on the web (calling APIs, manipulating coding, etc.)

Protecting yourself and your content online (ownership, permanence, etc.)

Mozilla

We think more pathways are better, so we plan to roll out our learning content in different formats, through different channels, including:


Through Events: We are building a set of 'kits' to enable people, communities and organizations to pick up our content, build on it and use it to run events such as learning labs or hack jams.

Dedicated Online Site: Ultimately, we will offer learning pathways through a new Mozilla webmaker site currently being developed.

Through our programs: We will roll our learning content through Open News, Popcorn and the Hive.

Through other channels: We hope the community will leverage our content and use through other channels as well.
